Food insecurity in the Bronx has risen to a new high, straining the emergency food sites in the area

By Aliyah Walker

NEW YORK - December 11, 2020

This year more than one in five Bronx residents (23.1%) are experiencing food insecurity. Food insecurity has always been an issue in the Bronx and has remained the hungriest borough for years.

Nicole Aber is the director of communication and development at Hunger Free America. She has a passion for raising awareness about food insecurity in New York City. At Hunger Free America her team is currently advocating for the next federal relief bill which will help provide funding to food pantries and soup kitchens.

Hunger Free America does an annual hunger survey and reports on their findings. This year in the Bronx they found out that 52.4% of the emergency food sites in the area have reported turning away people, reducing the amount of food distributed per person or limiting their hours of operation due to not having enough resources to support demand this year.

Aber stated that even with this funding, “There really is not going to be a short term fix. Were really expecting the effects of the crisis, specifically food insecurity to last at least a year if not longer.”

“Whenever there is a big economic crisis like this, we know that low income people are affected the most and the repercussions are longer lasting.”
